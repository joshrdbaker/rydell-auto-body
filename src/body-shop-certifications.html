I-CAR Gold Class

The gold class designator given by I-CAR is a recognition that our Grand Forks body shop is committed to the ongoing advancement of our employee's education. In order to be a I-CAR Gold certified shop the business must obtain and maintain a level of training that is relevant not only to the industry today, but also the training is directed as role-relevant. This ensures that every employee of our body shop has had extensive training in their particular role, whether that be an estimator, a metal or paint tech or a manager. To maintain the prestigious I-CAR Gold status the business and its employees must continue to expand their knowledge skill sets by completing additional courses that will cover advancements in repair procedures and manufacturer specific technologies.

According to the I-CAR website, it is estimated that less than 20 percent of repair shops currently meet the rigorous Gold Class standard and with the evolving technologies many shops will continue to fall behind in education that is relevant to the current OEM specifications.

Automotive Service Association (ASA)

ASA is another avenue that our technicians can further their education in the role that they hold. The goal of the ASA is to create a code of ethics that will give the consumer the confidence that when their vehicle is repaired by a shop that has ASA members will be the most knowledgeable in their field. ASA also works with manufacturers in order to maintain a high level of service given to their brand customers along with enforcing shops and employees keep in safety regulations in order to protect the customer and business alike. ASA holds meetings multiple times a year to discuss the changes in the technology and the automotive industry to include the world's largest collision repair event, the International Autobody Congress and Exposition (NACE).

“The Automotive Service Association (ASA) advances professionalism and excellence in the automotive repair industry through education, representation and member services.” — Automotive Service Association (ASA) Website

ProFirst Certified Collision Repair Facility

This certification is given to shops that meet a strict and independent standard based on multiple avenues that include; Honda specific training, facility and tools, customer satisfaction and an annual inspection. ProFirst is the official certification that is recognized by Honda and Acura that a auto body shop has met the standards to properly and safely repair their manufactured vehicles. As part of meeting this standard, our shop has access to all of Honda's service and repair information that ensures that your vehicle is repaired in accordance with the OEM's guidelines.

“Today, it takes a highly-qualified collision shop to properly repair a Honda or Acura automobile—one with training, skill, the proper tools and equipment and the latest repair information. It takes a shop dedicated to their craft—a ProFirst Certified shop.” — Honda
